The Silent Epidemic

Millions of Americans with cognitive disabilities face a profound communication crisis. This isn't just about words; it's a barrier to expressing thoughts, needs, and emotions, leading to social isolation and a diminished quality of life.

The Human Cost of Disconnection

The inability to communicate authentically has severe, cascading consequences across every facet of life.

  • ✓ Poorer Health Outcomes: Higher rates of chronic conditions and adverse medical events.
  • ✓ Social & Economic Disadvantage: Lower rates of employment and marriage.
  • ✓ Profound Social Exclusion: Loss of friendships and a decline in social activities.

A Link to Mental Health

The constant struggle to be understood takes a heavy toll. For instance, a significant portion of individuals with aphasia experience depression.

A Landscape of Incrementalism

Current Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) tools are built on an outdated paradigm. They are often slow, cognitively draining, and emotionally sterile, leading to high rates of user frustration and device abandonment.

The COGGI Solution

COGGI proposes a new architecture for human-centric communication, built on three synergistic pillars that create an adaptive, authentic, and cognitively efficient experience.

1. Personalization Engine

An on-device "communication twin" that learns the user's unique linguistic style, humor, and syntax. It runs entirely on the user's device, ensuring privacy, speed, and offline functionality.

2. Emotional Intelligence

Moves beyond simple emotion labels to generate language with genuine nuance. The system suggests phrases that carry the desired emotional weight, enabling authentic affective communication.

3. Context Awareness

Uses on-device sensors (GPS, calendar) to anticipate needs and proactively surface relevant vocabulary in dynamic visual scenes, dramatically reducing the cognitive load of searching for words.

A Framework for Inclusive Innovation

A technology this personal must be built *with* its community, not just *for* it. COGGI is founded on a commitment to participatory co-design and a robust ethical framework.
